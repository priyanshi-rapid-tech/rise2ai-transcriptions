# Lesson Summary: Introduction to Google AI Studio

### What are we building?
We're learning how to use **Google AI Studio** as a "Playground" for our AI apps. This tool allows you to test different Gemini models, fine-tune their behavior with system instructions, and then export the finalized code (Python, TypeScript, or REST) to use in your actual product.

### Quick thoughts before you start
*   How do you decide between using the "Pro" model (smart but expensive) and the "Flash" model (fast and cheap)?
*   Why is the "System Instruction" more powerful than just typing a standard question in the chat box?

### Expert Vocabulary
*   **System Instruction:** A permanent rule you set for the AI (e.g., "Always speak like a pirate") that stays active across all future questions in that session.
*   **Structured Output (JSON):** A setting that forces the AI to respond in a code-friendly format, making it easier for your app to "read" the data without human help.
*   **Temperature:** A setting that controls how "creative" or "random" the AI's responses are. High temperature = more creative; Low temperature = more factual and consistent.
*   **Thinking Mode:** A feature where the AI "reasons" through its answer internally before typing, often leading to better results for complex logic.

### Key Takeaways
1. **Flash is for Speed, Pro is for Brains.** If your task is simple (like a one-line recipe), use **Gemini Flash** to save money and time. Use **Gemini Pro** for complex reasoning or creative writing.
2. **Refine before you Export.** Don't write code from scratch. Use the playground to find the exact combination of system prompts and temperature that works, then hit "Get Code."
3. **JSON is your Bridge.** If you are building a real app, turn on "Structured Output." This ensures the AI sends back clean data that your app's code can easily process.
4. **Safety first.** AI Studio shows you exactly how many "Tokens" you are using and how much it would cost, helping you manage your budget before your app goes live.

### Reflection Questions

*   Why is it better to test your "Pirate Translator" in AI Studio instead of just testing it directly in your app's code?
