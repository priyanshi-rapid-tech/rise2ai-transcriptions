# Lesson Summary: VibePRD Day 5 — Save, Share & Ship

### What are we building?
We're taking our VibePRD app and making it permanent. Day 5 is about **Persistence** (saving work to a database), **Sharing** (generating public links for others), and **Shipping** (deploying the app to a live URL). We're moving from a temporary "experiment" to a real product that remembers your history and can be shared with your colleagues.

### Quick thoughts before you start
*   If you build a great PRD but can't save it or send it to your team, does the app have any real value?
*   How does an "Autosave" indicator (like in Google Docs) change how much a user trusts your software?

### Expert Vocabulary
*   **Persistence:** The technology that ensures your data (chat history and PRDs) is still there when you refresh the page or log in from a different computer.
*   **Replit Auth:** A built-in system that lets users log in using their Replit credentials, removing the need for you to build a complex login system.
*   **Public URL:** A permanent link (e.g., `yprd.replit.app`) that anyone on the internet can visit to see your tool.
*   **Export as Markdown:** A feature that converts your AI-generated document into a `.md` file, perfect for copy-pasting into Jira, GitHub, or Notion.

### Key Takeaways
1. **User Accounts are the Foundation.** By adding Replit Auth, you allow each user to have their own private "workspace." This ensures your PRDs aren't mixed up with anyone else's.
2. **Database vs. Design.** On Day 1, we just had a "picture" of a sidebar. On Day 5, that sidebar is "alive"—it pulls project titles from a Postgres database and allows you to switch between different ideas instantly.
3. **The "Share" Experience.** Professional software needs a clean "Read-Only" mode. When you share a PRD link, your colleague should see a beautiful document, not a messy chat window with AI bubbles.
4. **Deploying to Production.** The final step is clicking "Publish." This moves your app from the "Garage" (Development) to the "World" (Production), giving it a permanent home on the web.

### Reflection Questions
