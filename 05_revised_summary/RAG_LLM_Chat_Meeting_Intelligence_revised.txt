# Lesson Summary: Prism — Meeting Intelligence with RAG

### What are we building?
We're building **Prism**, a "Meeting Intelligence" platform that turns raw transcripts into professional insights. Instead of just summarizing, our app uses **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)** to extract specific commitments, friction points, and revenue figures, and allows you to chat directly with your call data with zero "hallucinations."

### Quick thoughts before you start
*   Why is a formal "Commitment Grid" (who promised what and when) more useful for a product manager than a long summary of a meeting?
*   What is the difference between an AI "guessing" an answer and an AI performing "RAG" to prove its answer using the transcript?

### Expert Vocabulary
*   **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation):** A process where an AI first "retrieves" specific facts from a document (like your transcript) and then uses those facts to generate a 100% accurate answer.
*   **Transcript Parsing:** Automatically breaking down a long block of text into speakers, timestamps, and key topics.
*   **Commitment Grid:** A structured list or table extracted from a conversation that clearly shows every deadline or promise made by the participants.
*   **Oracle Chat:** A specialized chat interface designed to answer questions *only* based on the uploaded data, refusing to "make things up" beyond the transcript.

### Key Takeaways
1. **Analyze, don't just Summarize.** A generic summary is often too vague. Build your app to look for "Friction Points" and "Next Steps" to provide real business value.
2. **Beware of "Mocks."** Sometimes AI builders will create a "fake" (mock) version of an app that doesn't actually connect to the AI. Always verify by asking, "What model are you actually using?" and "Show me your system prompt."
3. **Format for Readability.** Raw AI text can be messy. Ensure your app uses **Rich Text (Markdown)** so that bold headings, bullet points, and tables are easy for humans to read.
4. **Context verification.** A great intelligence tool should always provide "proof." Ask your AI to include the exact timestamp or quote from the transcript to verify its findings.

### Reflection Questions

*   If you were analyzing an earnings call, why would you want the AI to be "brief and punchy" rather than "verbose and long"?
