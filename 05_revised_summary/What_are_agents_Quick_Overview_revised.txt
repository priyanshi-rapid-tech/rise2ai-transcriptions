# Lesson Summary: What are Agents? (The Agentic Shift)

### What are we building?
We're learning the fundamental difference between a **Chatbot** and an **Agent**. While a chatbot is good at talking and giving information, an agent is designed to **take action** and achieve a specific goal. We are moving from giving instructions (telling the AI *how* to do every step) to defining intent (telling the AI *what* we want to achieve and letting it figure out the rest).

### Quick thoughts before you start
*   If a chatbot says "You should book a flight for $500," but an agent says "I just booked your flight for $480 and added it to your calendar," which one is more valuable to you?
*   How does giving an AI a "Goal" instead of a "Task" change the way the AI behaves?

### Expert Vocabulary
*   **The Agentic Shift:** The transition from software that only provides information to software that performs actual work (e.g., booking, filing, communicating).
*   **Instruction vs. Intent:** Instruction means you tell the AI every step ("Go to site X, click button Y"). Intent means you tell it the outcome ("Plan and book my family's 7-day trip to Paris").
*   **Agency:** The ability of an entity (software, person, or animal) to act with intention to reach a goal.
*   **LLM + Tool + Goal:** The "formula" for an agent. It uses an AI brain (LLM), connects it to the real world (Tools), and works toward a finish line (Goal).

### Key Takeaways
1. **Chatbots Propose, Agents Execute.** A chatbot will tell you what laptop you should buy. An agent will find the best deal, check your bank balance, and buy it for you.
2. **The "Loop" is Key.** An agent doesn't just stop after one answer. it will "loop through" different steps (checking calendars, talking to other agents, using APIs) until the goal is reached or it gives up.
3. **Actionable Outcomes.** In business, an agentic system doesn't just write a marketing plan; it actually coordinates with legal and marketing "sub-agents" to start launching the project.
4. **End-to-End Tasks.** Whether it's filing for PTO or arranging medical transportation, an agent handles the entire process from start to finish, rather than just helping you with one piece of the puzzle.

### Your Action Item
Think of a repetitive task you do every week (e.g., summarizing meeting notes and sending them to your boss). 
1. How would a **Chatbot** help you with this? 
2. How would an **Agent** do this differently? 
3. Identify one "Tool" (like your email or a CRM) that an agent would need to "take action" on this task.

### In short...
A chatbot is a consultant; an agent is an employee. Moving from "Information" to "Action" is the single biggest opportunity in AI today.
